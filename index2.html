What is it E-sport?

Cybersport is the leader among all sports activities in terms of engagement. Nielsen research testified that 90% of fans play by themselves. This is due to the fact that the core of the audience is young people.

1970-1980

Various sources call the world's first computer game competition the "Intergalactic Olympic Games" at Stanford University. In 1972, students competed in the Spacewar shooter for the top prize - a year's subscription to Rolling Stone magazine
8 years later, the largest at that time Space Invaders shooter championship took place. More than 10,000 players from all over America participated in it. This showed the demand for a new type of competition.
In the mid-80s, video games were brought into mass culture thanks to the advent of game consoles. For example, Nintendo Entertainment System (NES) boosted the industry with cult games Pac-Man, The Legend of Zelda, Super Mario Bros. and Donkey Kong.

1990-2010

In the 90s, the Internet appeared and paved the way for the movement of eSports. Players could now compete online. Game tournaments, professional leagues began to appear, federations were formed, and regular meetings of the participants of the most popular games were held. And the top teams won prizes totaling hundreds of thousands of dollars.
In the early 2000s, South Korea gave a big push to eSports. There, eSports has become extremely popular. Young people began to meet in computer clubs, hitting shopping centers and football stadiums. And in 1998, the real-time strategy Starcraft was released, which paved the way for eSports on TV. Dedicated gaming cable channels regularly covered StarCraft and Warcraft III competitions. And their revenue in 2007 exceeded $200 million.
In 2018, according to SuperData Research, the revenue of the global eSports market amounted to $1.5 billion. In 2022, according to forecasts, it will grow to $2.3 billion. ESports also brought a lot of profit to ordinary fans. For example, on bets through the mirror of the bookmaker's website.

E-sport in Ukraine

In Ukraine, eSports originated in the basements of computer clubs. In the early 2000s, video game competitions were run on pure enthusiasm. And the prize funds were only enough for lunch in an inexpensive cafe. You can find out what e-sports is in more detail on any information site on the Internet.
Ukrainian players took part in dozens of small competitions. And in 2001, the Samsung company decided to hold the World Cyber ​​Games selection in Ukraine. Selection rounds were held all over the country. A paid trip abroad, competitions with foreigners and a huge prize fund of hundreds of thousands of dollars awaited the winners.
After the World Cyber ​​Games, other major championships began to appear in Ukraine. For example, ASUS Open in 2004, which was held four times a year. These were system-forming competitions and they motivated Ukrainian players to develop.
At the end of 2006, the Dota game began to rapidly gain popularity in Ukraine and all CIS countries. And in 2011, the Ukrainian eSports team Natus Vincere won a record $1 million in the Dota 2 – The International championship. This attracted the attention of thousands of gamers and triggered a boom in Dota 2 championships.
And already in 2016, the company Valve, which created the Dota 2 game, decided to hold the first in the Ukrainian capital the main e-sports event for the entire CIS - the Dota 2 tournament - The Kiev Major. Prize fund – $3 million.

What is Ukraine's position in global e-Sports?

Oleksandr Kokhanovsky emphasized that Ukraine is among the ten most e-sports countries in the world. At the UPEA presentation held on September 17, he said that the eSports audience in Ukraine is 1.8 million people. 90% of them are men, the predominant age group is 16-36 years old.
The USA leads the rating, China is in second place, and South Korea is in third place. Next comes Germany, Switzerland, France, Canada, Great Britain, Ukraine is in ninth place and Russia is in tenth place.
However, there is a trend towards a downgrading of positions in the rating. In 2011, roci for a bag of prizes, took off at Esports tournaments, Ukraine took the third place. In 2015, it dropped to eight, and behind underbags 2019 rock - took only 28th position in the light rating.

How e-Sports is developing in the world

In 2019, the revenues of the eSports industry were estimated at $950 million, and the audience was estimated at 445 million people. Of these, 245 million were casual viewers, but 200 were eSports enthusiasts, the UPEA president said. These categories will increase significantly in a few years — up to 350 million and 295 million, respectively, by the end of 2023. At the same time, revenues will grow to $1.56 billion.
In 2019, the prize fund of The International Dota 2 tournament amounted to $34 million, and in 2020 it increased to $36 million. How it is planned to develop e-sports in Ukraine E-sports is definitely an innovative economy, definitely a creative economy and a 100% digital transformation of society, Oleksandr Kokhanovsky emphasized.
In addition, eSports is a huge opportunity to socialize a lot of people. For example, those who suffer from post-traumatic syndrome, because "we are in a warring country", said Minister of the Cabinet of Ministers Oleg Nemchinov.
Therefore, the newly created association UPEA has set itself the task of bringing Ukraine into the top five countries in e-sports.
In the countries, where e-sports segment is the most developed, wines penetrate into the most popular schools and institutions. For example, in Denmark, eSports has already been upgraded to some high school programs.
